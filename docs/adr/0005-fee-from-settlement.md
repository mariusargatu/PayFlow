# ADR-0005: Platform fee is drawn from settlement, not the merchant

**Date:** 2026-07-02
**Status:** Accepted.

## Context

A full codebase review found a contradiction among three of the system's own rules. The original capture ledger credited the merchant the full captured amount `C`, then debited the flat fee from the merchant (merchant -> platform_fees), so the merchant netted `C - fee`. Refunds are bounded by `captured_amount` (the full `C`) and fees are not returned. So a full refund debited the merchant the full `C` while the merchant held only `C - fee`, driving the merchant balance to `-fee` and breaching INV-3 (no merchant, holds, or platform_fees balance is ever negative). The three rules (fee taken from the merchant, refund up to captured, merchant never negative) cannot all hold. It was reproduced with the default fee: capture 100, refund 100, merchant balance -30.

## Decision

The `capture_fee` ledger pair is debited from `external_settlement`, not the merchant: `external_settlement -> platform_fees`. The merchant is credited and keeps the full captured amount `C`. A full refund (`merchant -> external_settlement`, amount `C`) therefore returns the merchant to zero, and no internal account goes negative. Only `external_settlement`, which represents the outside world and is explicitly allowed to go negative, absorbs the fee. Global conservation (INV-4) still holds.

Consequences of the change:
- The "capture amount must exceed the fee" rule is retained but reclassified: it was a solvency guard (keep the merchant net non negative), now it is a platform business rule (no capture smaller than the fee it generates).
- The committed agent discovered Layer 1 specs encoded the old merchant balance formula (`merchant = captures - fees - refunds`). They were regenerated by re running discovery against the corrected system and re accepted, and the mutation baseline was recomputed. This is the project's own loop: a review found a specification bug, the specification was corrected, and the agent rediscovered the properties.

## Alternatives rejected

- **Cap refund at the merchant's net** (`captured - fees - refunded`): a full refund would return less than the customer paid, which is wrong for a payment system.
- **Relax INV-3 to allow merchant negative**: most faithful to card processing where the merchant eats the fee, but it weakens the headline "no internal account ever goes negative" invariant that the demonstration rests on.
